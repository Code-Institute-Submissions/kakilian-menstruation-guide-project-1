That's why I've created an easy-to-understand guide to help navigate the experience, providing support, clearing up myths, and emphasizing safety and hygiene.

What is menstruation?

Menstruation is the normal vaginal bleeding that happens in cycles. In earlier years, cycles typically range from 21 to 45 days, while in young adult life it's usually 21 to 31 days, continuing until around age 51. It can start anywhere from age 10 to 15 and varies based on individual body and diet.

"Menstrual blood and tissue flow from your uterus through a small opening in your cervix and pass out of your body through your vagina. The uterus lining builds up to prepare for pregnancy." (Source: Women's Health)

Symptoms?

Common symptoms, known as PMS (Premenstrual Syndrome), include breast tenderness, bloating, headaches, mood swings, depression, anxiety, anger, irritability, lower back pains, and diarrhea. These can occur one or two weeks before each period and usually ease up when the bleeding starts due to hormonal changes.

Myths Debunked:

  • Fainting: Fainting during PMS or periods is unlikely due to minimal blood loss.
  • Sports: You can still do sports during your period. Listen to your body, as exercise can help with flow and pain relief.
  • Hair Washing: Washing your hair is perfectly fine for hygiene. Daily showers or baths are recommended for cleanliness and mood improvement.
  • Privacy: Having your period isn't a secret. It's a normal bodily process that shouldn't make you feel ashamed or abnormal.

Guidance

What can I do? - self awareness

There are two main things, I have broken them down to: Non-medical and medication. Always try the non-medical things first, to see which fits your body best and when.

Self-Help Tips: Use warm bottles, blankets, drink water, reduce salt, alcohol, and caffeine intake, take warm baths, rest, and consider supplements like calcium, Vitamin D, and Iron based on your diet.

Medication: Non-prescription anti-inflammatory drugs like Naproxen or Ibuprofen can help with symptoms but should be taken under adult or doctor supervision.

Materials for Hygiene Management

Choose from sanitary pads, menstrual underwear, or tampons based on your needs and comfort level. Make sure to dispose of products properly and change them regularly.
